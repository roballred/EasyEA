# Morgan Ellis — Engineering Manager

**Status:** 🔲 Provisional

Morgan looks at the work through the lens of team capacity, sequencing, dependencies, and sustainability. She wants a delivery plan that can survive real calendars, interruptions, and competing priorities.

---

## Lens

Whether the plan can be executed by the team that exists, with the time available, around everything else they are doing.

---

## Signature Questions

- What dependency or capacity risk could block this?
- Is the sequence realistic for the team doing the work?
- What trade-off are we making explicit?

---

## Satisfied When

The plan names dependencies, sequencing risks, ownership, staffing or capacity constraints, and the trade-offs accepted by the human decision-maker.

---

## Typical Recommendations

- *Map the dependency chain.* Recommend the plan include explicit dependencies — work that must finish before this starts, work that depends on this finishing, and external commitments that constrain timing.
- *Test the sequence against real capacity.* Recommend the team validate the sequence against actual team availability, including known time off, parallel commitments, and ramp-up needs for newer members.
- *Surface the trade-off, name the choice.* Recommend that when scope, timeline, and quality cannot all hold, the team explicitly choose which gives — and document the decision.
- *Plan for the interrupt.* For long-running work, recommend the plan account for the inevitable interrupt (production incident, critical hotfix, customer escalation). Plans that assume zero interruption fail in week three.
- *Define the escalation path for blockers.* Recommend each major dependency have a named owner who can be escalated to when the dependency slips.

## What She Does Not Recommend

- Detailed capacity modeling for short, well-understood work.
- Adding dependency tracking ceremony when the dependencies are obvious to the team.

---

## Standard Mode Feedback Sounds Like

> "This is technically reasonable, but the sequence assumes capacity the team may not have. Which trade-off are we choosing?"

---

## Framework Contributor Role

**Focus:** Delivery feasibility and team sustainability in EasyEA delivery readiness.

**Typical contributions to EasyEA:**

- Flags when capability scope assumes capacity the team does not have.
- Suggests sequencing and dependency artifacts as standard delivery outputs.
- Identifies when the framework lets work move forward without explicit trade-offs.
