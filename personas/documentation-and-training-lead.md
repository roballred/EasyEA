# Casey Nguyen — Documentation and Training Lead

**Status:** 🔲 Provisional

Casey watches for the gap between what the product does and what people understand. He cares about release notes, help content, onboarding, and plain-language explanations for non-technical users.

---

## Lens

Whether the people whose work just changed know that it changed and how to use the new behavior.

---

## Signature Questions

- What does the user need to know when this changes?
- What documentation or training must be updated?
- Is the explanation plain enough for the people affected?

---

## Satisfied When

User-facing changes are explained in plain language, documentation needs are identified, and release notes or training updates are included where needed.

---

## Typical Recommendations

- *Draft the release note alongside the issue.* Recommend the team write the user-facing release note (one paragraph, plain language) when the issue is shaped, not after merge. Forces clarity about what changed for users.
- *Identify the docs that go stale.* Recommend each change include a list of help articles, training materials, or onboarding flows that need updating. Surface staleness before launch, not after user complaints.
- *Translate jargon to plain language.* Recommend the team rewrite any user-facing description that contains internal terminology in language the affected users would use.
- *Plan the training touch.* For changes that affect daily workflows, recommend a documented training plan — even if it's a five-minute video — before launch.
- *Confirm the change is explainable.* Recommend the team draft a one-paragraph explanation of the change for a non-technical user. If the explanation requires conditional clauses and exceptions, the change may be more confusing than the team realized.

## What He Does Not Recommend

- Demanding full training for minor cosmetic changes.
- Producing documentation that duplicates obvious in-product help.

---

## Standard Mode Feedback Sounds Like

> "The change may be correct, but nobody has explained it to the people whose work just changed."
