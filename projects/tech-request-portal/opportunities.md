## Opportunity Details

### OPP-01 — Single Structured Submission Channel
**Value:** Marcus stops asking who to contact. Dana receives requests with the context she needs. Back-and-forth before review can begin drops significantly.
**What makes it feasible:** A structured form is a low-complexity starting point. No complex integrations required in Phase 1.
**Risk:** If the form asks too many questions, Marcus abandons it and goes back to email. Form length must be minimal.

---

### OPP-02 — Existing Technology Catalog with Search
**Value:** Eliminates the most frustrating scenario for Marcus — waiting weeks to be told the tool already exists. Reduces Dana's review load for requests that could be self-served.
**What makes it feasible:** A searchable list of approved tools. The challenge is initial population — someone has to build the catalog.
**Risk:** If the catalog is incomplete or out of date, Marcus loses trust in it and stops checking. Catalog quality must be maintained.

---

### OPP-03 — Real-Time Request Status
**Value:** Marcus stops emailing for status. Dana's inbox stops filling with follow-up questions. Trust in the process improves when the process is visible.
**What makes it feasible:** Status tracking is a standard feature of any request management approach. No novel complexity.
**Risk:** Low. The main risk is that status labels are unclear — "under review" needs to mean something specific.

---

### OPP-04 — Structured Review with Criteria and Precedent
**Value:** Dana applies consistent criteria to every request. She can find previous decisions in seconds instead of reconstructing from memory. Review time for standard requests drops.
**What makes it feasible:** Criteria can be defined as a checklist. Precedent lookup requires a searchable decision record (OPP-05).
**Risk:** Criteria that are too rigid create bottlenecks. They must be guidelines, not hard gates, for most request types.

---

### OPP-05 — Decision Record with Rationale
**Value:** Every decision is searchable and citable. Priya can show auditors exactly why a technology was approved. Dana avoids re-evaluating the same question twice.
**What makes it feasible:** A natural output of OPP-04 — structured review produces structured decisions.
**Risk:** If recording rationale adds significant time to Dana's review, it will be skipped. Must be brief and structured — not a free-text essay.

---

### OPP-06 — Portfolio Intelligence
**Value:** Priya sees what has been approved, what patterns are emerging, and where duplication exists — without asking anyone.
**What makes it feasible:** Reporting on structured data from OPP-01 through OPP-05.
**Risk:** No value until there is sufficient volume of recorded decisions. Phase 1 must come first.

---

## Phasing Recommendation

**Phase 1 — Foundation (highest value, highest feasibility):**
OPP-01, OPP-03, OPP-05

Gets Marcus submitting cleanly, gives him status visibility, and starts building the decision record. Delivers immediate value to all three personas.

**Phase 2 — Review Quality:**
OPP-02, OPP-04

Adds the technology catalog and structured review criteria. Requires the foundation from Phase 1 to work well.

**Phase 3 — Intelligence:**
OPP-06

Portfolio reporting becomes valuable once Phase 1 and 2 have generated sufficient data.
