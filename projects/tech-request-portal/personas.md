# User Personas — Technology Request Portal

> Personas are living artifacts. Update them as real user research, feedback, and testing reveal new insights. These are starting hypotheses — not final definitions.

Every capability, design decision, and business rule must trace back to at least one persona's pain point or goal.

---

## Marcus — The Frustrated Project Manager

**System role:** Requester
**Capabilities served:** CAP-01, CAP-02, CAP-04

Marcus manages delivery projects across the organization. He regularly needs tools, licenses, or new technology to support his teams — but getting approval is unpredictable and slow.

**Pain points**
- Doesn't know where to submit a request or who to contact — it changes depending on the type of request
- Submits requests and hears nothing for weeks; follows up by email to get any status
- Has been told "we already have something for that" after waiting three weeks — information he could have used on day one
- Different requests for similar tools get different answers with no explanation

**Goals**
- Submit a request quickly without having to figure out the right process first
- Know where his request is and when to expect a decision
- Find out upfront if something already exists before going through the full process

> **Critical insight:** Marcus's willingness to use the portal depends entirely on whether it saves him time compared to just asking someone directly. If the first experience is slow or confusing, he goes back to email.

## Dana — The Overwhelmed IT Reviewer

**System role:** Reviewer
**Capabilities served:** CAP-03, CAP-04, CAP-05, CAP-06

Dana is a Central IT architect who reviews technology requests alongside her regular work. Reviews are unstructured, arrive through multiple channels, and frequently ask the same questions she has answered before.

**Pain points**
- Requests arrive by email, ticket, and chat — no single queue to work from
- No standard format means every request requires back-and-forth just to gather the basics
- Spends time re-evaluating requests for tools that were already approved or rejected
- Cannot easily check whether a similar request was handled before, or how it was decided
- Gets blamed for delays even when the requester hasn't provided what she needs

**Goals**
- See all pending requests in one place with enough context to act on them
- Apply consistent criteria to every review without starting from scratch each time
- Find previous decisions quickly to avoid re-doing work
- Close out a standard request in a single session without chasing the requester

> **Critical insight:** Dana is the throughput bottleneck. If the portal doesn't reduce her review effort, approval times will not improve regardless of how easy submission is for Marcus.

## Priya — The Compliance-Conscious IT Director

**System role:** Admin / Approver
**Capabilities served:** CAP-05, CAP-06, CAP-07

Priya leads Central IT and is accountable for technology decisions across the organization. She needs confidence that approvals are consistent, traceable, and defensible — without being involved in every individual review.

**Pain points**
- Cannot see a summary of what has been approved, rejected, or is pending without asking someone
- Has no way to verify that reviewers are applying consistent criteria
- Gets surprised by technology choices that were never submitted for review
- When audited, has no clean record of why a technology was approved or rejected

**Goals**
- Dashboard view of portfolio health — what's pending, what's been approved, what patterns are emerging
- Confidence that the review process is being followed consistently
- A decision record she can point to when a technology choice is questioned
- Visibility into technology sprawl — how many tools are doing the same job

> **Critical insight:** Priya doesn't use the portal daily, but her trust in it determines whether it becomes the official process or a parallel system that people route around.

## The Value Chain

```
Marcus submits a complete, well-structured request
    ↓
Dana can review it in a single session with full context
    ↓
Decisions are consistent, traceable, and recorded
    ↓
Priya has visibility into the portfolio and confidence in the process
    ↓
Technology decisions are faster, more consistent, and defensible
```
