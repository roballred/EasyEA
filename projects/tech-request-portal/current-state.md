## How a Technology Request Works Today

### Submission
1. Marcus identifies a tool his team needs
2. He asks around to find out who to contact — answer varies by request type
3. He sends an email to Central IT (or submits a generic helpdesk ticket with no structure)
4. The email lands in a shared inbox or gets routed manually to a reviewer

### Review
5. Dana sees the request — usually missing context (no business case, no budget info, no security considerations)
6. She emails Marcus for more information — waiting begins
7. Marcus responds days later (or not at all)
8. Dana evaluates the request from memory and judgment — no standard criteria, no check against previous decisions
9. She may escalate to Priya informally if the request is large or unusual

### Decision
10. Dana replies by email with approval or rejection — one line, no rationale recorded
11. Marcus receives the answer but has no documentation of why
12. The decision lives in Dana's email, not in any system

### No decision path
- Marcus never hears back and submits again, or gives up and finds a workaround
- A tool gets purchased without going through the process at all

## Friction Points

| Where | What Breaks Down | Who Feels It |
|-------|-----------------|-------------|
| Submission | No standard channel — Marcus asks around every time | Marcus |
| Submission | No structured format — requests arrive as free-text emails | Dana |
| Review | Missing information requires back-and-forth before review can start | Dana, Marcus |
| Review | No check for existing tools — duplicates get approved | Dana, Marcus |
| Review | No access to previous decisions — Dana re-evaluates from scratch | Dana |
| Decision | No decision record — rationale lives in email or not at all | Dana, Priya |
| Status | Marcus has no way to check status — must email to ask | Marcus |
| Oversight | Priya cannot see what is pending or what has been decided without asking Dana | Priya |

---

## Data Flow

- **What's needed to review:** Business justification, cost, security considerations, existing alternatives considered
- **Where it comes from:** Marcus, manually, after back-and-forth
- **Where decisions go:** Email threads — not searchable, not shared, not retained systematically
- **What's lost:** Rationale, patterns, precedent, portfolio view

---

## Workarounds People Have Invented

- Teams purchase SaaS tools on a credit card without submitting a request
- Managers call Priya directly to fast-track approvals
- Reviewers keep personal spreadsheets of past decisions for reference
- Marcus submits to multiple channels simultaneously hoping one gets picked up faster
