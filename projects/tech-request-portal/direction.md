# Direction — Technology Request Portal

**Version:** v1
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-30
**EasyEA Step:** 1 — Set the Direction

---

## Business Goals

| Goal | Why It Matters |
|------|---------------|
| Reduce time from technology request to decision | Teams are waiting weeks for approval on requests that should take days. Delays slow delivery and erode trust in Central IT. |
| Make technology decisions visible and consistent | Similar requests get different answers depending on who you ask. Decisions need to be traceable and reproducible. |
| Give requesters clarity on where their request stands | Today, requests go in and disappear. People follow up by email or give up entirely. |

---

## Problems to Solve

- There is no single place to submit a technology request — requests go by email, ticket, and informal conversation
- Requesters have no visibility into approval status or expected timeline
- Reviewers have no structured way to evaluate requests against standards
- Duplicate technology gets approved because there is no check for existing capabilities
- Decisions are not recorded, so the same question gets answered differently over time

---

## Guiding Principles

- Self-service where possible — reduce the need to ask Central IT for status
- Structured but not bureaucratic — enough process to be consistent, not so much that it slows things down
- Decisions must be explainable — every approval or rejection needs a reason

---

## Constraints

| Constraint | Type | Impact |
|-----------|------|--------|
| Must integrate with existing identity provider | Technology | No separate login — use what already exists |
| No budget for a commercial product in the first phase | Budget | Build on existing platforms or use open tooling |
| Must not add review burden on Central IT staff | Organizational | The portal must make reviews faster, not create more work |

---

## What Success Looks Like

- A team can submit a technology request in under 10 minutes without asking for help
- Requesters can check the status of their request at any time without emailing anyone
- Central IT can process a standard request in 2 business days or less
- No two requests for similar technology get opposite answers without explanation

---

## What Is Out of Scope

- Procurement or contract management — this handles the decision, not the purchase
- Infrastructure provisioning — approval is out of scope for actual deployment
- Budget approval workflows — financial approval is a separate process
